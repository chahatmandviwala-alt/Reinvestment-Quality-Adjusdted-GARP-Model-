About this model

The idea. A plain PEG treats all growth as equal. But growth costs capital, and ROIC tells you the price. qPEG discounts the PEG for the excess return a business earns over its hurdle: qPEG = PEG × (h/ROIC)^k. Below ~1.0 is reasonably priced; above ~1.25 is expensive.

Post-tax ROIC. The hurdle is a post-tax cost of capital, so the return is put on the same basis: ROIC = ROCE × (1 − average effective tax). ROCE comes from your screener; the tax rate is averaged over the three years entered.

The exponent k controls how much credit the high return earns. The idealised value-driver anchor is k = g/(ROIC − g), but that assumes the firm reinvests at ROIC forever. The tool scales it by how much of the implied reinvestment is actually deployed (from your cash flows, with acquisitions weighted at 50% since acquired returns usually trail organic ones), then caps it at linear. k is the one judgemental input — the suggested value is a defensible starting point; override it for durability or fade.

Fair value is the price at the midpoint of the fair band (qPEG 1.0–1.25). Everything is a screening lens on the numbers you enter — not investment advice. Cyclical businesses, where ROCE and growth swing, need their inputs normalised to mid-cycle first.
